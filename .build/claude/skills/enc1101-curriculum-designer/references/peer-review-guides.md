# Peer Review Guides

## General Peer Review Protocol

1. Read the entire draft without marking anything
2. Read again with the guide, making notes
3. Write your feedbackâ€”be specific, kind, and constructive
4. Discuss with the writer (if time allows)

**Feedback Principles:**
- Describe what you see before evaluating
- Ask questions rather than giving commands
- Point to specific passages
- Balance strengths and areas for growth
- Focus on higher-order concerns first (ideas, organization) before lower-order (grammar)

## Literacy Narrative Peer Review

**Reader:** _________________ **Writer:** _________________

### First Impressions
After reading once, what stands out most? What's the main insight or "so what" of this narrative?

### Reflection & Meaning
1. What does the writer seem to have learned about themselves as a reader/writer?
2. Where could the writer dig deeper into the significance of their experiences?
3. Are there moments that seem important but aren't fully explored?

### Storytelling
1. Which scenes or moments are most vivid? What makes them work?
2. Where would you like more concrete details, dialogue, or sensory description?
3. Does the narrative have a clear arc (beginning, middle, end)? Where might the pacing be adjusted?

### Voice & Style
1. How would you describe the writer's voice? Is it consistent?
2. Point to one sentence you found particularly effective. Why does it work?

### One Priority for Revision
What's the ONE thing you'd encourage the writer to focus on in revision?

## Rhetorical Analysis Peer Review

**Reader:** _________________ **Writer:** _________________

### Thesis Check
1. Copy the thesis statement here:
2. Is it clear what text is being analyzed?
3. Does it make a claim about HOW the text works rhetorically (not just WHAT it says)?
4. Is the thesis arguable (could someone disagree)?

### Analysis Quality
1. Which rhetorical strategies does the writer analyze? (List them)
2. Where is the analysis strongest? What makes it effective?
3. Where does the draft slip into summary rather than analysis?
4. Does the writer explain WHY the strategies work (effect on audience)?

### Evidence Use
1. Are quotes smoothly integrated (signal phrases, context)?
2. Does every quote have analysis attached to it?
3. Point to one place where evidence use could be improved:

### Organization
1. Does each body paragraph focus on one main analytical point?
2. Are transitions between paragraphs clear?
3. Does the conclusion do more than repeat the introduction?

### One Priority for Revision
What's the ONE thing you'd encourage the writer to focus on in revision?

## Research-Based Argument Peer Review

**Reader:** _________________ **Writer:** _________________

### Argument Assessment
1. What is the writer's main argument/thesis? Copy it here:
2. Is it clear, specific, and contestable?
3. What's at stake in this argument? Why does it matter?

### Source Evaluation
1. List the sources you can identify in this draft:
2. Do the sources seem credible and appropriate for the argument?
3. Where might the writer need additional evidence or different types of sources?

### Source Integration
1. Are sources introduced with signal phrases?
2. Does the writer's voice remain dominant, or do sources take over?
3. Point to one place where a source is well-integrated and one where integration could improve:

### Counterarguments
1. Does the writer acknowledge opposing viewpoints?
2. How effectively are counterarguments addressed?
3. What counterargument might strengthen the paper if addressed?

### Organization & Flow
1. Is the argument structure logical?
2. Does each paragraph clearly advance the argument?
3. Where did you get confused or lost?

### One Priority for Revision
What's the ONE thing you'd encourage the writer to focus on in revision?

## Reflective Portfolio Peer Review

**Reader:** _________________ **Writer:** _________________

### Reflection Quality
1. Does the writer demonstrate genuine reflection on their growth?
2. What specific skills or insights does the writer claim to have developed?
3. Is there evidence from their work to support these claims?

### Portfolio Coherence
1. Do the selected pieces effectively demonstrate the writer's growth/abilities?
2. Does the reflection connect meaningfully to the included work?
3. What might strengthen the portfolio's overall narrative?

### Self-Assessment
1. Does the writer accurately assess their strengths and areas for growth?
2. Are they specific about what they've learned and how?
3. Do they connect their learning to future writing situations?

### One Strength to Celebrate
What's the strongest aspect of this portfolio and reflection?
