# Interview Follow-Up Templates

Templates for thank-you notes, follow-up emails, and post-interview communication.

## Thank-You Emails

### Standard Thank-You (Send Within 24 Hours)

```
Subject: Thank you — [Role] interview

Hi [Interviewer Name],

Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today about the
[Role] position. I enjoyed learning about [specific topic discussed]
and the team's work on [project or initiative mentioned].

Our conversation reinforced my enthusiasm for the role, particularly
[specific aspect that excited you — tie to something they said].

I'm confident my experience in [relevant skill/area] would allow me
to contribute meaningfully to [specific goal or challenge discussed].

Please don't hesitate to reach out if you need any additional
information. I look forward to hearing about next steps.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### Thank-You After Panel Interview

```
Subject: Thank you — [Role] discussion

Hi [Panel Lead / Hiring Manager Name],

Thank you for organizing today's conversation. I appreciated the
chance to meet with the team and hear different perspectives on
[the role / the project / the challenge].

I was particularly energized by [Name]'s insights on [topic] and
[Name]'s question about [topic] — it's clear this team values
[quality you observed: collaboration, rigor, creativity, etc.].

I'm excited about the possibility of contributing to [specific
initiative or goal], and I'm happy to provide any additional
materials or references.

Thank you again to everyone involved.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

[Optional: If you had meaningful 1-on-1 time with individual
panelists, send brief personalized notes to each.]
```

### Thank-You After Technical Interview

```
Subject: Thanks for the technical discussion

Hi [Interviewer Name],

Thank you for the technical interview today. I enjoyed working
through the [problem type — system design, coding challenge,
architecture discussion] with you.

After our conversation, I thought more about [specific problem or
question], and [brief additional insight or approach you thought of].
I appreciated your guidance when I was working through [specific
moment in the interview].

The technical challenges your team is tackling in [area] are
exactly the kind of work I find most engaging. I'd welcome the
chance to contribute.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## Status Follow-Ups

### Checking In (After Stated Timeline Passes)

```
Subject: [Role] — checking in

Hi [Recruiter / Hiring Manager Name],

I hope you're doing well. I wanted to follow up on the [Role]
position — you mentioned a decision was expected around [date/week].

I remain very interested in the opportunity and would welcome any
updates when you have a chance.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

### Second Follow-Up (7-10 Days After First)

```
Subject: Re: [Role] — checking in

Hi [Name],

Just floating this back up. I understand hiring timelines can shift,
so no pressure at all — just wanted to reiterate my interest in
the [Role].

Happy to provide any additional information that would be helpful.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### After Extended Silence (3+ Weeks, Final Check)

```
Subject: Re: [Role] position

Hi [Name],

I wanted to reach out one more time regarding the [Role] position.
I understand you may still be in process or the timeline may have
changed.

If the role is still open, I'd love to remain in consideration.
If it's been filled or the search is paused, I completely understand
and appreciate the time you and the team spent with me.

Either way, I wish you and the team the best.

Warm regards,
[Your Name]
```

## Responding to Offers

### Requesting Time to Consider

```
Subject: Re: [Role] offer

Hi [Name],

Thank you so much for the offer — I'm genuinely excited about
the opportunity to join [Company] as [Role].

I'd like to take a few days to review the full package carefully.
Would it be possible to share my decision by [date, typically
3-5 business days out]?

In the meantime, if there's a written offer letter or benefits
summary I should review, I'd appreciate receiving that.

Thank you again,
[Your Name]
```

### Accepting an Offer

```
Subject: Re: [Role] offer — accepted

Hi [Name],

I'm thrilled to accept the offer for [Role] at [Company]. Thank
you for this opportunity and for the thoughtful process throughout.

I'm looking forward to starting on [date] and contributing to
[something specific about the team or mission].

Please let me know about any onboarding steps, paperwork, or
logistics I should take care of before my start date.

Thank you again,
[Your Name]
```

### Declining an Offer

```
Subject: Re: [Role] offer

Hi [Name],

Thank you very much for the offer to join [Company] as [Role].
I've given it careful thought, and after considering my current
priorities, I've decided to pursue a different direction.

This was not an easy decision — I have great respect for the team
and the work you're doing. I hope our paths cross again.

I wish you and the team continued success.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

## Post-Rejection Communication

### Responding Gracefully to a Rejection

```
Subject: Re: [Role] update

Hi [Name],

Thank you for letting me know. While I'm disappointed, I appreciate
the time you and the team spent with me throughout the process.

I genuinely enjoyed learning about [Company]'s work on [topic] and
would welcome the chance to be considered for future openings that
align with my background.

If you're open to it, I'd appreciate any brief feedback on my
candidacy — it would help me in my search.

Wishing you and the team all the best,
[Your Name]
```

### Requesting Feedback After Rejection

```
Subject: Re: [Role] — quick question

Hi [Name],

Thank you again for considering me for [Role]. As I continue my
search, any feedback you could share about my interview performance
or candidacy would be genuinely helpful.

Even a sentence or two would be appreciated — no pressure at all
if company policy doesn't allow it.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

## Timing Guidelines

| Situation | When to Send | Format |
|-----------|--------------|--------|
| Thank you after interview | Within 24 hours | Email |
| Follow-up on timeline | 1-2 days after stated date | Email |
| Second follow-up | 7-10 days after first | Email |
| Final check-in | 3-4 weeks after interview | Email |
| Offer response | Within stated deadline | Email, then call |
| Post-rejection thanks | Within 48 hours | Email |

### General Tips

- Proofread every email twice before sending
- Reference specific conversation details to stand out
- Keep emails concise — 4-6 sentences for thank-yous, 2-3 for follow-ups
- Never express frustration about delays or ghosting
- If multiple interviewers, personalize each note with something unique from that conversation
- Save all correspondence for your records
