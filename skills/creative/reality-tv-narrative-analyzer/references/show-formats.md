# Reality TV Show Format Breakdowns

## Competition Formats

### Survivor (CBS, 2000-)

**Format:**
- 16-20 contestants on remote location
- Tribal immunity challenges → tribal council eliminations
- Individual immunity in merge phase
- Final tribal council: jury votes for winner

**Episode Structure:**
1. Reward challenge (sometimes combined)
2. Camp life / strategy
3. Immunity challenge
4. Scramble period
5. Tribal council

**Key Narrative Elements:**
- Tribal divisions (pre-merge)
- Idol hunts and blindsides
- Alliance betrayals
- Final 3 fire-making (modern seasons)

**Edit Patterns:**
- Winner typically gets consistent, positive content
- Decoy winners get inflated late-game edits
- Eliminated players spike in content their boot episode

---

### Big Brother (CBS, 2000-)

**Format:**
- 16 houseguests in surveilled house
- Head of Household competition weekly
- Nomination → Veto → Eviction cycle
- Jury votes for winner among final 2-3

**Episode Structure (3x/week):**
- Sunday: Nominations
- Wednesday: Veto competition
- Thursday: Live eviction

**Key Narrative Elements:**
- HOH room reveals
- Diary room confessionals
- Alliance meetings
- Backdoor strategies

**Edit Patterns:**
- Houseguests get episode focus based on game relevance
- Showmances get dedicated segments
- Comp beasts get challenge-focused edits

---

### The Bachelor/Bachelorette (ABC, 2002-)

**Format:**
- 1 lead, 25-30 contestants
- Rose ceremonies eliminate contestants
- Hometown dates → Fantasy suites → Final rose

**Episode Structure:**
1. Group date
2. One-on-one date
3. Cocktail party drama
4. Rose ceremony

**Key Narrative Elements:**
- "Journey" framing
- Villain of the season
- Front-runner vs. underdog tension
- Dramatic exits

**Edit Patterns:**
- Winner gets balanced, romantic edit
- Runner-up often gets tragic edit
- Villains get extensive confessional time
- "Bachelor in Paradise" candidates get memorable moments

---

### RuPaul's Drag Race (Logo/VH1, 2009-)

**Format:**
- 12-16 drag queens
- Weekly runway + main challenge
- Lip sync for your life elimination

**Episode Structure:**
1. Mini challenge
2. Main challenge work
3. Runway presentation
4. Judges' critiques
5. Lip sync elimination

**Key Narrative Elements:**
- Werkroom discussions
- Mirror messages from eliminated queens
- Reading challenges
- Snatch Game (celebrity impersonation)

**Edit Patterns:**
- Narrator queens get lots of confessional time
- Winners show versatility across challenges
- Villain edits often lead to fan backlash
- "Redemption" arcs for returning queens

---

### MasterChef / Top Chef

**Format:**
- Amateur (MasterChef) or professional (Top Chef) cooks
- Challenge → Judging → Elimination
- Progressively difficult challenges

**Episode Structure:**
1. Mystery box / quickfire challenge
2. Main elimination challenge
3. Tasting and judging
4. Elimination

**Key Narrative Elements:**
- Backstory packages
- Time pressure drama
- Mentor relationships with judges
- Restaurant Wars (Top Chef)

---

## Docusoap Formats

### Real Housewives (Bravo, 2006-)

**Format:**
- Ensemble cast (5-7 women)
- Follows daily lives with manufactured events
- Reunion episodes cap each season

**Episode Structure:**
- Multiple storylines intercut
- Confessional commentary
- Group events for conflict potential
- Cliffhanger endings

**Key Narrative Elements:**
- "Taglines" establish character
- Feuds drive seasons
- Lifestyle porn (homes, fashion)
- Fourth wall acknowledgment in confessionals

**Edit Patterns:**
- Heroes and villains shift season to season
- Fan favorites protected by edit
- Reunion "receipts" shown for vindication

---

### Keeping Up with the Kardashians (E!, 2007-2021)

**Format:**
- Single extended family
- Mix of manufactured storylines and real events
- Cross-promotion with family businesses

**Key Narrative Elements:**
- Family loyalty vs. individual ambition
- Relationship drama
- Business ventures
- Generational dynamics

---

## Dating Formats

### Love Island (ITV/various, 2015-)

**Format:**
- Couples in villa
- Recoupling ceremonies
- New islanders disrupt dynamics
- Public voting influences outcomes

**Episode Structure (daily):**
- Morning recap
- Daytime activities / challenges
- Evening drama
- Night: Recoupling or elimination

**Key Narrative Elements:**
- "Cracking on" / coupling dynamics
- Casa Amor test
- Dumping ceremonies
- "I've got a text!" announcements

---

### The Circle (Netflix, 2020-)

**Format:**
- Isolated players communicate only through social media app
- Catfishing allowed
- Players rate each other → eliminations
- Blocked players can meet someone before leaving

**Key Narrative Elements:**
- Authentic vs. catfish tension
- Alliance building through screens
- "Circle chat" group dynamics
- Final reveals

---

## Adventure/Endurance Formats

### The Amazing Race (CBS, 2001-)

**Format:**
- Teams of 2 race around world
- Route markers, Detours, Roadblocks
- Last team to pit stop eliminated

**Episode Structure:**
1. Teams depart in order
2. Travel to destination
3. Challenges (Detour choices)
4. Race to pit stop

**Key Narrative Elements:**
- Relationship under pressure
- Cultural encounters
- Navigation drama
- U-turns and yields (sabotage)

---

### Alone (History, 2015-)

**Format:**
- 10 survivalists in wilderness
- Self-filmed
- Last person remaining wins
- Voluntary "tap outs"

**Key Narrative Elements:**
- Isolation psychology
- Survival skills demonstration
- Weight loss / medical checks
- "Tap out" decisions

**Edit Patterns:**
- Winner typically shown building sustainable camp
- Early eliminations get less developed arcs
- Mental health struggles shown sympathetically
