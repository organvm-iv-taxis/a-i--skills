# WPA Outcomes Statement Alignment Guide

## Rhetorical Knowledge

**By the end of first-year composition, students should:**

- Learn and use key rhetorical concepts through analyzing and composing a variety of texts
- Gain experience reading and composing in several genres with attention to rhetorical situations
- Develop facility in responding to different situations and contexts
- Understand and use a variety of technologies to address audiences
- Match form to situation, readers, contexts, and purposes

**Assignment Alignment:**
- Rhetorical Analysis assignment directly addresses these outcomes
- All assignments should specify audience and purpose explicitly
- Include genre awareness in assignment prompts

## Critical Thinking, Reading, and Composing

**By the end of first-year composition, students should:**

- Use composing and reading for inquiry, learning, thinking, and communicating
- Read a diverse range of texts with attention to rhetorical strategies
- Locate and evaluate primary and secondary research materials
- Use strategies for generating, revising, editing, and proofreading
- Learn to compose ethically by crediting sources

**Assignment Alignment:**
- Research-Based Argument develops source evaluation skills
- All assignments should include multiple drafts
- Require integration of sources with proper citation

## Processes

**By the end of first-year composition, students should:**

- Develop a writing project through multiple drafts
- Develop flexible strategies for reading, drafting, reviewing, revising, rewriting, and editing
- Use composing processes and tools as means to discover and reconsider ideas
- Experience collaborative and social aspects of writing processes
- Learn to give and act on productive feedback
- Adapt composing processes for variety of technologies and modalities

**Assignment Alignment:**
- Build mandatory process checkpoints into every assignment
- Include peer review for all major assignments
- Require reflection on process alongside final products

## Knowledge of Conventions

**By the end of first-year composition, students should:**

- Develop knowledge of linguistic structures, conventions, and citation systems
- Understand why genre conventions differ and adapt writing accordingly
- Gain experience negotiating conventions in multiple genres
- Learn discipline-specific and professional conventions as appropriate

**Assignment Alignment:**
- Teach MLA/APA as rhetorical choices, not arbitrary rules
- Address genre conventions explicitly in assignment prompts
- Include editing workshops focused on patterns of error
