# Rubric Templates

Ready-to-use rubrics for common assessment scenarios.

## Analytic Rubric Template (4-Point Scale)

### Generic Essay Rubric

| Criterion | Excellent (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
|-----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Thesis/Argument** | Clear, specific, arguable thesis that directly addresses prompt | Thesis present and clear, addresses prompt | Thesis unclear, too broad, or partially addresses prompt | No identifiable thesis or thesis unrelated to prompt |
| **Evidence** | Multiple relevant, well-chosen examples with smooth integration | Adequate evidence with minor integration issues | Limited evidence or poorly integrated | Little to no supporting evidence |
| **Analysis** | Sophisticated analysis connecting evidence to argument throughout | Analysis present but could probe deeper in places | More summary than analysis; surface-level connections | Minimal or no analysis of evidence |
| **Organization** | Clear, logical structure with effective transitions between ideas | Mostly organized with functional transitions | Some organizational issues; transitions unclear | No clear structure; difficult to follow |
| **Clarity/Mechanics** | Nearly error-free; clear, varied sentences | Few errors; meaning is clear | Some errors impede meaning in places | Frequent errors significantly impede reading |

**Total: ___/20**

## Analytic Rubric Template (3-Point Scale)

### Presentation/Speaking Rubric

| Criterion | Exceeds (3) | Meets (2) | Approaching (1) |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Content Knowledge** | Demonstrates thorough understanding; handles questions confidently | Demonstrates adequate understanding; answers most questions | Gaps in understanding evident; struggles with questions |
| **Organization** | Clear introduction, body, conclusion; logical flow | Structure present but some transitions unclear | Disorganized; hard to follow main points |
| **Delivery** | Confident posture, eye contact, varied tone; engages audience | Adequate delivery; some nervousness; mostly reads from notes | Reads entirely from notes; minimal audience engagement |
| **Visual Aids** | Slides enhance presentation; clear, professional, not overcrowded | Slides support content but have minor issues | Slides distract or are hard to read |
| **Time Management** | Within time limit with appropriate pacing | Slightly over/under; some rushing or padding | Significantly over/under time |

**Total: ___/15**

## Holistic Rubric Template

### Quick Assessment Scale

**5 - Exemplary**
Work exceeds expectations. Demonstrates sophisticated understanding, original thinking, and polished execution. Could serve as a model for others.

**4 - Strong**
Work meets all expectations and exceeds some. Shows solid understanding with minor areas for refinement. Ready for next steps.

**3 - Proficient**
Work meets expectations. Demonstrates adequate understanding and execution. Some revision needed but fundamentally sound.

**2 - Developing**
Work partially meets expectations. Shows emerging understanding but significant revision needed. Key elements missing or underdeveloped.

**1 - Beginning**
Work does not meet expectations. Fundamental misunderstanding or incomplete. Substantial reworking required.

## Peer Review Rubric

### Feedback Quality Rubric

| Criterion | Strong (3) | Adequate (2) | Needs Work (1) |
|-----------|------------|--------------|----------------|
| **Specificity** | Points to exact passages; gives concrete examples | Generally specific but some vague comments | Vague; "good job" or "needs work" without examples |
| **Actionability** | Offers clear, doable suggestions for improvement | Some suggestions but not always clear how to implement | No suggestions or unrealistic ones |
| **Balance** | Notes both strengths and growth areas | Mostly critical or mostly praise | One-sided feedback |
| **Tone** | Respectful, constructive, encouraging | Mostly constructive; minor tone issues | Harsh, dismissive, or unhelpful |
| **Depth** | Addresses higher-order concerns (ideas, structure) | Some attention to ideas; mostly surface-level | Focuses only on grammar/spelling |

## Rubric Design Checklist

Before using a rubric, verify:

- [ ] Criteria align with assignment learning objectives
- [ ] Descriptors use observable, measurable language
- [ ] Levels are distinguishable (no overlapping descriptions)
- [ ] Rubric shared with students BEFORE assignment
- [ ] Language avoids vague terms ("good," "adequate," "appropriate")
- [ ] Point values reflect relative importance of criteria
- [ ] Format matches purpose (analytic for detailed feedback, holistic for quick assessment)
