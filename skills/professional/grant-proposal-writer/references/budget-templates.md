## Budget Justification Template

### Personnel Justification

**[Name], Principal Investigator** ($[Amount] requested)
[Name] will devote [X] months ([X]% effort) to this project. As PI, [Name] will [specific responsibilities: design experiments, oversee team, analyze data, write publications]. [Name]'s qualifications include [relevant experience]. Salary is based on the institutional rate of $[Amount] annually.

**[Name/TBD], [Position]** ($[Amount] requested)
[The position/person] will dedicate [X] months to [specific tasks]. This position is essential because [justification]. [If TBD: We will recruit someone with [qualifications]].

### Fringe Benefits

Fringe benefits are calculated at institutional rates: [X]% for faculty, [X]% for postdocs, [X]% for graduate students, [X]% for hourly employees. Rates include [health insurance, retirement, FICA, etc.].

### Equipment

**[Item Name]** ($[Amount])
This [equipment] is necessary for [specific research activity]. It will be used to [function]. We obtained quotes from [vendor] showing $[Amount]. This equipment will remain at [institution] after the grant period for continued research.

### Supplies

**Laboratory Supplies** ($[Amount])
Consumables include [specific items] for [X experiments/samples]. Cost calculated as [breakdown: X items x $Y each = $Z].

**Computing/Software** ($[Amount])
Software licenses for [specific software] required for [data analysis/modeling/etc.]. Annual license costs $[Amount] from [vendor].

### Travel

**Domestic Conference** ($[Amount] x [N] trips)
Attendance at [conference name] is essential for [presenting findings, networking, learning latest methods]. Estimate: $[X] airfare + $[Y] lodging (N nights x $rate) + $[Z] registration + $[W] per diem.

**Research Site Visit** ($[Amount] x [N] trips)
Travel to [location] is necessary for [data collection, collaboration, etc.]. Estimate: [breakdown].

### Other Direct Costs

**Participant Support** ($[Amount])
Stipends of $[X] each for [N] participants to [compensate for time, cover travel, etc.]. Essential for [recruitment, ethical compensation, etc.].

**Publication Costs** ($[Amount])
Open access publication fees for [N] anticipated publications in [journals]. Average cost $[X] per article.

**Subaward to [Institution]** ($[Amount])
[Brief description of subcontracted work and why this institution]. See detailed subaward budget attached.

### Indirect Costs

Indirect costs calculated at [X]%, the federally negotiated rate for [on-campus/off-campus] sponsored research at [Institution]. Rate agreement dated [Date] is included as an appendix.

## Multi-Year Budget Escalation

### Standard Escalation Rates

| Category | Annual Increase | Justification |
|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Faculty salaries | 3% | Per institutional policy |
| Staff salaries | 3% | Per institutional policy |
| Graduate stipends | 2-3% | Competitive adjustment |
| Fringe benefits | 0-2% | Based on projected rates |
| Supplies | 2-3% | Inflation adjustment |
| Travel | 3% | Inflation adjustment |

### Year-Over-Year Calculation

```
Year 2 = Year 1 x (1 + escalation rate)
Year 3 = Year 2 x (1 + escalation rate)

Example:
Year 1 salary: $60,000
Year 2 salary: $60,000 x 1.03 = $61,800
Year 3 salary: $61,800 x 1.03 = $63,654
```

## Common Budget Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Math errors | Triple-check all calculations |
| Missing fringe | Always include benefits |
| Vague line items | Be specific about what/why |
| Inflation ignored | Apply escalation to multi-year |
| No justification | Every line needs rationale |
| Wrong indirect rate | Verify current negotiated rate |
| Equipment vs. supplies | Know funder's threshold ($5K typical) |
| Exceeds limit | Check funder's caps |
