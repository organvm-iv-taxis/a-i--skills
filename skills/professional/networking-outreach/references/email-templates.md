# Networking Email Templates

Templates for professional outreach and relationship building.

## Cold Outreach

### Informational Interview Request

```
Subject: Quick question about [their specialty] at [Company]

Hi [Name],

I came across your [post about X / talk on Y / work at Z] and
[specific thing that resonated or impressed you].

I'm [your role/situation] exploring [what you're interested in],
and your perspective on [specific topic] would be invaluable.

Would you have 15-20 minutes for a call? I'd love to hear about
[specific question or topic]. Happy to work around your schedule.

No worries if you're too busy—I know these requests add up.

Best,
[Your name]
[LinkedIn URL or one-line bio]
```

### Cold Email to Hiring Manager

```
Subject: [Role Name] - [Your unique angle]

Hi [Name],

I saw [Company] is hiring for [Role]. I'm [brief relevant
background—2-3 accomplishments max].

What excites me about this role:
- [Specific thing about the role/company/team]
- [How your background connects]

I've already applied through [channel], but wanted to reach out
directly. Would you have a few minutes to chat about the role?

Thanks for considering,
[Your name]
[LinkedIn URL]
```

### Reconnecting with a Dormant Connection

```
Subject: Been a while!

Hi [Name],

It's been a while since we [met at X / worked together at Y /
connected about Z]. I hope you're doing well!

I've been following [their work/company] and was impressed by
[recent accomplishment or news].

I'm currently [what you're up to now], and I'd love to catch up
if you have time. No agenda—just curious how things are going
on your end.

Free for a quick call or coffee in the next few weeks?

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Following Up

### First Follow-Up (Day 4-5)

```
Subject: Re: [Original subject]

Hi [Name],

Just floating this back up in case it got buried.

[Add one new sentence of value—a relevant article, insight,
or connection to recent news about them/their company.]

[Your name]
```

### Final Follow-Up (Day 10-14)

```
Subject: Re: [Original subject]

Hi [Name],

I'll assume the timing isn't right—totally understand.

If things change down the road, I'd still love to connect
about [topic].

Wishing you all the best,
[Your name]
```

## Post-Informational Interview

### Thank You Email (Send Within 24 Hours)

```
Subject: Thanks for the conversation

Hi [Name],

Thank you so much for taking the time to speak with me today.
I really appreciated your insights on [specific topic discussed].

Your advice about [specific piece of advice] especially
resonated—I'm going to [how you'll act on it].

I'll definitely [follow through on something discussed—read
that book, reach out to that person, try that approach].

Thanks again, and I'll keep you posted on how things go.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Sharing Something Valuable (Week 2-4)

```
Subject: Thought of our conversation

Hi [Name],

I came across this [article/resource/tool] and thought of our
conversation about [topic].

[Link or brief description]

[One sentence on why it's relevant to them]

Hope it's useful!

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Progress Update (Month 1-2)

```
Subject: Quick update

Hi [Name],

I wanted to share a quick update since we spoke.

I [took your advice and X happened / explored Y and learned Z /
made progress on W].

Thank you again for the guidance—it really helped.

Hope things are going well on your end!

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Requesting Referrals

### Asking for a Warm Introduction

```
Subject: Intro request: [Person or Company]

Hi [Name],

I noticed you're connected to [Person] at [Company] on LinkedIn.
I'm [reason for interest—applying for role, exploring industry,
researching something].

Would you be comfortable making an introduction? Totally
understand if not—I know that's a big ask.

If helpful, here's a forwardable blurb:

---
[Your name] is a [role/background] who [1-2 sentence value prop].
They're interested in [specific topic] and would love to learn
about [specific question].
---

Thanks for considering,
[Your name]
```

### Asking to Be Referred for a Job

```
Subject: [Company] referral?

Hi [Name],

I saw there's a [Role] opening at [Company]. Based on
[our previous conversation / my background in X], I think
it could be a great fit.

Would you be open to referring me? I've already prepared
my application and can share my resume if helpful.

If you need more info or want to catch up first, I'm happy
to chat.

Thanks for considering—I really appreciate it.

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Subject Line Formulas

### High-Performing Patterns

| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Mutual connection | "Alex Chen suggested I reach out" |
| Specific reference | "Your talk at [Conference]" |
| Direct question | "Quick question about [topic]" |
| Shared context | "Fellow [School] alum in [field]" |
| Value offer | "Resource on [topic you discussed]" |
| Brevity | "15 min?" |

### Subject Lines to Avoid

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| "Hi" or "Hello" | Too vague, gets deleted |
| "Important" | Feels spammy |
| "Request for [X]" | Sounds transactional |
| Long subjects | Get truncated |
| All caps | Looks unprofessional |
| No subject | Easily missed |

## Writing Tips

### Opening Lines That Work

| Instead of | Try |
|------------|-----|
| "I hope this email finds you well" | [Specific reference to their work] |
| "My name is..." | [Start with why you're reaching out] |
| "I'm sure you're busy, but..." | [Confident, direct ask] |

### Closing Lines That Work

| Instead of | Try |
|------------|-----|
| "Let me know if you're interested" | "Would [specific time] work for a call?" |
| "I look forward to hearing from you" | "No worries if the timing isn't right" |
| "Please feel free to reach out" | [Specific next step] |

### Length Guidelines

| Email Type | Ideal Length |
|------------|--------------|
| Cold outreach | 75-125 words |
| Follow-up | 30-50 words |
| Thank you | 75-100 words |
| Referral request | 100-150 words |
